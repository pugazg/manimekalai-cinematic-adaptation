# 03B — Indra Festival Source Dossier

Production Research Edition 0.1

## Purpose

This dossier defines how the Indra Festival will be portrayed on screen. It is a research framework, not a final historical claim.

## Foundational Rule

Begin with the evidence of Manimekalai and related early Tamil literature. Do not begin with later Purāṇic, medieval, colonial or modern representations of Indra.

## Evidence Hierarchy

1. [TEXT] Manimekalai
2. [CROSS] Silappathikaram, Pattinappalai and the Sangam corpus
3. [HISTORY] Archaeology and historians
4. [INTERPRETATION] Cinematic reconstruction

## Working Reconstruction

The festival functions simultaneously as:

- Royal proclamation
- Civic renewal
- Appeal for rain and prosperity
- Public decoration
- Merchant gathering
- Military ceremony
- Philosophical assembly
- Religious plurality
- Social celebration

## Visual Language

Avoid depicting the festival as a single temple ritual. Instead, show the entire city participating:

- fresh sand spread across streets;
- decorated gateways;
- markets overflowing;
- scholars debating;
- musicians rehearsing;
- harbour activity increasing;
- heralds announcing the festival;
- elephants and horses in ceremonial roles.

## What We Must Avoid

- Later calendar-art iconography
- Massive medieval temple complexes
- Uniform theology
- Modern religious labels
- Supernatural spectacle without narrative purpose

## Questions for Classical Scholars

1. Relationship between Indra Vizha in Manimekalai and Silappathikaram.
2. Early Tamil meaning of the festival.
3. Relation to rain, kingship and agriculture.
4. Ritual sequence.
5. Material culture.
6. Music and performance.
7. Role of Buddhist participation.

## Storyboard Anchor Sequence

1. Dawn.
2. Festival proclamation.
3. Streets transformed.
4. Philosophical gathering.
5. Merchant quarter.
6. Madhavi's household.
7. Manimekalai enters the city.
8. Festival splendour contrasted with hidden suffering.
