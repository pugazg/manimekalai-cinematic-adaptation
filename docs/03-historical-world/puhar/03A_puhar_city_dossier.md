# 03A — Puhar City Dossier

Production Research Edition 0.1

Purpose: Establish a production-grade reconstruction of Puhar (Kaveripoompattinam) for screenplay, storyboard, production design and VFX.

## Core Design Principle

Do not depict Puhar as a later medieval stone-temple city. Build it as a living river–sea port of the early historic Tamil world using literary evidence, archaeology and cautious interpretation.

## City Identity

- Maritime capital
- Chola administrative centre
- Cosmopolitan trading hub
- Festival city
- Centre of artistic performance
- Home of multiple religious traditions and philosophical debate

## Street Language

Every district should possess distinct sounds, smells, colours and occupations. The audience should immediately recognise where they are without captions.

## Festival Rule

Indra Vizha is treated as an early Tamil civic-sacral institution centred on prosperity, rain, public order and plural participation. Do not import later iconography without primary evidence.

## Architecture

Timber, brick, plaster, tiled roofs, thatch, courtyards, warehouses, pavilions and river infrastructure dominate. Avoid monumental later-period gopurams.

7. Manimekalai walking through layered social worlds.

## Director's Visual Thesis

Puhar is not merely beautiful. It is a city convinced of its own prosperity. The emotional journey of the film begins when Manimekalai discovers the people whom that prosperity has failed.
