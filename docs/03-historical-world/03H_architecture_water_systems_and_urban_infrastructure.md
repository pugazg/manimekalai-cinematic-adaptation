# 03H — Architecture, Water Systems and Urban Infrastructure

## Production Research Dossier 0.1

**Scope:** Puhar, Vanji, Kanchi, Manipallavam support spaces, halls, gardens, prisons, feeding institutions, water systems and civic infrastructure.

**Status:** Working research edition. Literary descriptions, archaeological evidence and production reconstruction are kept separate.

---

## 1. Core rule

Ancient cities in *Manimekalai* are not reconstructed from one surviving building type. The adaptation separates:

1. **Textual architecture** — places named or described in the epic.
2. **Archaeological architecture** — excavated walls, wells, wharves, bricks, reservoirs and comparable structures.
3. **Cinematic architecture** — coherent environments created for storytelling.

A poetic description of splendour is not a construction drawing.

---

# 2. Puhar — the maritime city

Puhar is the strongest architectural anchor because it combines literary city description with archaeological context.

**[TEXT]** Puhar contains festival spaces, gardens, performance areas, temples, streets, royal spaces, feeding locations and places of confinement.

**[HISTORY]** Archaeology at Poompuhar records excavation at Kizharveli and Dharmakulam. The Kizharveli structure with brick walls, platforms and wooden posts has been interpreted as a wharf. citeturn0search0

## Production reconstruction

Puhar should appear as a water-dependent port:

- river landing areas;
- warehouses near cargo movement;
- artisan districts;
- performance and festival zones;
- residential streets with social differences;
- public spaces shaped by shade, water and movement.

The harbour is distributed, not a single monumental stone quay.

---

# 3. Water systems

Water is treated as infrastructure, not decoration.

Possible visual elements:

- wells;
- ponds;
- tanks;
- channels;
- river access points;
- storage vessels;
- drainage routes.

**CAUTION:** A surviving ring well does not automatically prove its exact function. It may relate to water supply, domestic use or another purpose depending on context.

Poompuhar research has reported ring wells, brick structures and intertidal remains, but each feature requires archaeological interpretation rather than cinematic assumption. citeturn0search12

# 4. Public feeding institutions

The Amudhasurabhi scenes require architecture of care.

The feeding space should include:

- access routes;
- water availability;
- serving areas;
- storage and cleaning;
- spaces for different recipients.

The miracle does not replace labour, organisation or dignity.

The architecture must communicate an institution, not a fantasy food stage.

---

# 5. Prison transformation

The prison-to-care transformation is one of the epic's strongest architectural ideas.

The adaptation should show transformation of purpose:

Before:
- confinement;
- guarded movement;
- restricted access.

After:
- kitchens;
- lodging;
- feeding;
- service roles;
- public benefit.

A new institution is created, not merely a decorated prison.

---

# 6. Chakravala / cremation-ground landscape

The Chakravalakkottam episode requires a boundary landscape:

- walls;
- entrances;
- ritual movement;
- monks or ascetics;
- proximity to death and reflection.

It must not become a horror-fantasy cemetery. The literary function is philosophical transition and refuge.

---

# 7. Vanji

Vanji should differ from Puhar.

Visual identity:

- inland river connections;
- administrative spaces;
- learning environments;
- trade routes rather than harbour spectacle.

Karur remains a provisional production anchor, not an absolute identification.

---

# 8. Kanchi

Kanchi requires a dedicated later dossier because its urban development spans multiple periods.

Binding rule:

Later Pallava monuments must not automatically represent *Manimekalai*'s earlier world.

The adaptation should focus on:

- famine-affected streets;
- royal decision spaces;
- teaching spaces;
- relief infrastructure;
- public feeding.

---

# 9. Production prohibitions

Do not use:

- later temple towers as default ancient scenery;
- medieval palace assumptions;
- fantasy stone megacities;
- modern urban planning grids;
- unsupported sewer, aqueduct or harbour claims.

---

# 10. Architecture principle

The city of *Manimekalai* is defined less by monuments and more by relationships:

water connects labour,
food connects ethics,
streets connect communities,
and institutions reveal political choices.
