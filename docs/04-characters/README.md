# Character Bible

Character dossiers convert the research archive into actor-facing, screenwriter-facing and continuity-controlled performance documents.

Each dossier must separate:

- textual evidence;
- cross-textual inheritance;
- historical context;
- controlled psychological interpretation;
- unresolved questions;
- and binding production decisions.

## Character-dossier rule

A literary character is not a modern clinical case or a collection of plot functions. Psychological and performance interpretations must remain traceable to evidence and clearly labelled when the poem is silent.

Every completed dossier should include:

- dramatic identity and contradiction;
- life-stage and casting cautions;
- emotional and intellectual arcs;
- voice and movement systems;
- costume and prop continuity;
- relationship map;
- act-by-act character spine;
- actor preparation;
- evidence crosswalk;
- binding decisions;
- and unresolved specialist questions.
