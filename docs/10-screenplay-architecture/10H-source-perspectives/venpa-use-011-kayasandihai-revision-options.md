## Current scene function

The existing scene gives Kayasandihai a careful physical recovery:

1. she initially eats as though food may disappear;
2. hunger releases gradually rather than through comic or spectacular miracle;
3. breath, posture and attention return;
4. she says `Enough`;
5. Manimekalai asks what she needs now;
6. the action line states that she considers a future beyond the next mouthful.

The scene's ethical premise is strong. Its incompleteness is that Kayasandihai never answers and then disappears from the active narrative.

## Primary-source correction

The primary epic gives Kayasandihai more agency after hunger relief than the original proposal assumed. After being fed, she:

- explains her history and the cause of her condition;
- gives Manimekalai useful direction and knowledge;
- identifies the public refuge associated with continuing service;
- resumes her own course rather than remaining only a recipient, disguise or memory in Kanchanan's story.

The current screenplay has already placed Scene `#33#` at Ulaga Aravi. It therefore cannot literally reproduce Kayasandihai directing Manimekalai toward that location without breaking chronology or repeating information the feature has already established.

The revision should preserve the **function** of her source-supported counsel and onward movement without inventing an unsupported destination or expanding the branch into a second narrative.

## Option A — Revise to compressed counsel

**Decision form:** `REVISE`  
**Recommendation:** Preferred

### Proposed English continuation

```text
KAYASANDIHAI
Water. Rest. Then I will tell you where hunger waits beyond this courtyard.
```

### Proposed Tamil continuation

```text
காயசண்டிகை
தண்ணீர். ஓய்வு. பிறகு இந்த முற்றத்துக்கு அப்பால் பசி எங்கே காத்திருக்கிறது என்று சொல்கிறேன்.
```

### Placement

Immediately after:

```text
MANIMEKALAI
What do you need now?
```

and its Tamil equivalent.

### Why this option is stronger

- It begins with immediate bodily needs rather than turning recovery instantly into service.
- It restores Kayasandihai as a knower and guide, not only a recipient.
- It compresses the primary epic's counsel function into the feature's existing geography.
- It allows her experience of hunger and travel to alter Manimekalai's next understanding.
- It does not send her automatically to Kanchanan or define her through marriage.
- It does not invent her death, disappearance or final metaphysical destination.

### Risks

- `where hunger waits` is screenplay synthesis rather than direct source language.
- The line may duplicate the logistics already established in Scene `#32#` unless performed as lived geographical knowledge rather than policy advice.
- The Tamil wording requires spoken review; `இந்த முற்றத்துக்கு அப்பால்` may be shortened in performance if it sounds explanatory.

## Recommendation

Choose **Option A**, subject to source-edition, terminology and spoken-Tamil review.

The recommended revision is not a literal recovery of Kayasandihai's primary speech. It is a functional compression that preserves three source-supported movements:

1. recovery restores thought and speech;
2. the former recipient becomes a source of knowledge;
3. she retains a course beyond the feeding scene.

Option B is acceptable only if strict scene economy outweighs the loss of her counsellor function. Option C is safer than inserting a misleading source claim, but it leaves the original agency gap unresolved.
