## Existing screenplay movement

Scene `#23#` establishes compassion before miracle. Aputhiran gathers ordinary food and serves:

- an elderly woman who cannot stand in a public queue;
- a labourer with an injured hand;
- a child who feeds a stray dog before eating.

Only after everyone has eaten does he eat what remains.

Scene `#24#` then gives him the inexhaustible vessel and stages Indra's offer:

```text
INDRA
Ask what heaven may give you.

APUTHIRAN
Give food to those you left hungry.
```

The screenplay immediately interprets the refusal as courageous while identifying the first flicker of pride and Aputhiran's growing need to measure his worth through being needed.

The scene's conceptual architecture is strong. The question is whether the refusal needs one visible service action before the interpretive narration begins.

## Source finding

The primary epic directly connects Aputhiran's refusal of heavenly reward with the superior ethical value of feeding the hungry. The checked source does not require a newly introduced elderly traveller at the precise moment of refusal.

Bharathidasan similarly emphasises Aputhiran's hunger-relief ethic. The valuable element is the return from divine recognition to service—not the identity of a newly invented recipient.

## Option A — Introduce an elderly traveller

**Decision form:** retain original proposal

### English beat

```text
An elderly traveller appears at the edge of the feeding space, holding an empty leaf cup.

Aputhiran turns from Indra before heaven can answer. He serves the traveller.
```

### Tamil beat

```text
உணவளிப்பு இடத்தின் ஓரத்தில் காலியான இலைக் கிண்ணத்துடன் ஒரு முதிய பயணி தோன்றுகிறார்.

வானம் பதில் சொல்லும் முன்பே ஆபுத்திரன் இந்திரனிடமிருந்து திரும்பி, அந்தப் பயணிக்கு உணவு அளிக்கிறான்.
```

### Strengths

- Gives the refusal immediate human consequence.
- Visually displaces Indra from the centre.
- Makes Aputhiran act before the narration judges him.

### Weaknesses

- Repeats the elderly-recipient function already established in Scene `#23#`.
- Introduces a new person only to prove a theme already present.
- The phrase `before heaven can answer` may over-stage the divine confrontation.
- The traveller risks feeling like a symbolic prop rather than a continuing person.

## Option B — Return to the last waiting recipient

**Decision form:** `REVISE — SELECTED`  
**Recommendation:** Preferred

### English beat

```text
Aputhiran turns away from Indra.

He lifts the vessel and serves the last person still waiting at the edge of the feeding space.
```

### Tamil beat

```text
ஆபுத்திரன் இந்திரனிடமிருந்து திரும்புகிறான்.

பாத்திரத்தை எடுத்துக் கொண்டு உணவளிப்பு இடத்தின் ஓரத்தில் இன்னும் காத்திருக்கும் கடைசி மனிதருக்கு உணவு அளிக்கிறான்.
```

### Placement

Immediately after:

```text
APUTHIRAN
Give food to those you left hungry.
```

and before:

```text
The refusal is courageous. Pride flickers inside it.
```

### Strengths

- Completes the refusal through service rather than speech alone.
- Uses the existing feeding-space population instead of inventing a new symbolic recipient.
- Preserves continuity with Scene `#23#`.
- Turns Aputhiran's body away from divine recognition and toward a waiting person.
- Allows courage and pride to coexist: he genuinely serves, but the narration may still identify his emerging need to be indispensable.
- Keeps hunger—not cosmology—as the scene's centre.

### Risks

- `the last person still waiting` is screenplay synthesis and must not be described as recovered source action.
- The Tamil phrase `கடைசி மனிதர்` may sound administratively literal or gender-marked; spoken-Tamil review should consider `இன்னும் காத்திருக்கும் ஒருவருக்கு`.
- Blocking must avoid making the recipient faceless or merely illustrative.

### Preferred Tamil performance alternative

```text
ஆபுத்திரன் இந்திரனிடமிருந்து திரும்புகிறான்.

பாத்திரத்தை எடுத்துக் கொண்டு ஓரத்தில் இன்னும் காத்திருக்கும் ஒருவருக்கு உணவு அளிக்கிறான்.
```

This version is less rigid and may perform more naturally.

## Option C — Keep the scene verbal

**Decision form:** reject insertion

Retain the current dialogue and narration without an added action.

### Strengths

- The source ethic is already clear.
- Scene `#23#` has already proven that Aputhiran serves before miracle.
- The current compression is efficient.
- The narration's critique of pride arrives without sentimental reinforcement.

### Weaknesses

- The refusal remains a declaration addressed to heaven.
- Indra continues to occupy the centre of the visible encounter.
- The scene tells the audience that service matters instead of allowing Aputhiran to demonstrate it at the moment of reward.

## Explicit Stage 3 decision

Select **Option B — return to the last waiting recipient**.

The selected action is narrower and more continuous than the original elderly-traveller proposal. It preserves the primary ethical core while avoiding duplicate character function.

`VENPA-USE-016` remains `PROPOSED`. The explicit selection fixes the revision path but does not authorise screenplay insertion.
