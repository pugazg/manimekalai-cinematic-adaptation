## Existing screenplay movement

The current screenplay establishes:

1. Aadhirai controls the household threshold and stores;
2. no crowd witnesses the beginning;
3. she contributes one handful of grain;
4. she states that food kept behind a door protects only one house;
5. her wish crosses the threshold;
6. the public feeding system then forms around Manimekalai;
7. water, queues, translation, cleaning, animal feeding and safe access become part of the work.

The ethical architecture is already strong. The weakness is material scale and causal clarity: one handful of uncooked grain makes the first human gift symbolic, while the audience may perceive the vessel's fullness as primarily miraculous rather than as generosity transformed through service.

## Source comparison

### Primary epic

The checked Chapter 16 movement directly supports:

- Aadhirai receiving Manimekalai;
- Aadhirai placing food in the vessel;
- the vessel becoming full;
- Manimekalai continuing into public feeding.

The primary passage supports a substantial human offering and fullness, but the checked witness does not securely establish all of Bharathidasan's culinary detail.

### Bharathidasan perspective

The Venpa explicitly intensifies the domestic food action through:

- cooked rice;
- curry;
- ghee;
- serving with both hands until the vessel is full;
- Manimekalai then serving everyone.

This makes household labour and generosity visibly precede inexhaustibility.

## Governing adaptation question

How much culinary specificity should the feature borrow from Bharathidasan before visual scan verification and food-history review are complete?

### Scene #32 reveal

```text

```

Tamil:

```text
ஆதிரை இட்ட சோறும் கறியும் முதல் சிலருக்கு மணிமேகலை பரிமாறுகிறாள்.
```

### Strengths

- Most closely preserves Bharathidasan's domestic and sensory emphasis.
- Makes women's household labour visible rather than abstract.
- Gives the first fullness to human generosity before miracle.
- Provides a clear visual bridge from private food to public feeding.

### Risks

- Curry and ghee are later-perspective details unless confirmed in the selected primary Tamil edition.
- `கறி` requires culinary and historical-register review; modern audiences may narrow its meaning incorrectly.
- Ghee catching the light may become decorative spectacle if shot as sacred glamour rather than ordinary food.
- The visual may imply that the vessel is already full before its miraculous continuity is discovered, which requires precise blocking.

## Option C — Retain the current handful of grain

**Decision form:** `REJECT BOTH PROPOSALS`

Retain the existing Scene `#31#` action and make no Scene `#32#` abundance insert.

### Strengths

- No new source or terminology risk.
- Preserves the intimate, symbolic threshold action.
- Avoids additional explanatory beats.

### Weaknesses

- Understates Aadhirai's material contribution.
- Makes household labour nearly invisible.
- Leaves fullness primarily to the miraculous object.
- Weakens the causal order present in both primary and Venpa movements.
- Misses the opportunity to establish that the first abundance begins with an ordinary person opening household stores.

## Linked decision recommendation

Choose **Option B — source-conservative prepared household offering** for the active proposal path.

This decision should revise both records together:

- `VENPA-USE-007`: prepared household food visibly fills the vessel through Aadhirai's action;
- `VENPA-USE-008`: only after Manimekalai serves the first portions does she discover that the level has not fallen.

Option B preserves the strongest ethical and causal insight:

1. food is prepared by human labour;
2. Aadhirai opens private resources;
3. Manimekalai receives the act as trust rather than tribute;
4. portions are served to actual recipients;
5. continuing abundance is discovered;
6. logistics and collective organisation remain necessary.

The cooked-rice, curry and ghee detail from Option A should remain a **conditional production-design note** pending visual page confirmation, Tamil primary-edition comparison and food-history review. It should not be embedded in screenplay action before those gates pass.

## Required visual and performance rule

The sequence must not be staged as:

- Aadhirai worshipping an object while recipients disappear;
- a magical flash at the threshold;
- food glamour detached from hunger;
- a crowd praising Aadhirai before the private ethical choice occurs;
- Manimekalai alone producing the feeding system.

The intended order is:

```text
household labour -> voluntary giving -> first portions -> continuing abundance -> collective logistics
```
