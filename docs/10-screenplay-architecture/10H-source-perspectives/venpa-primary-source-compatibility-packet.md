## Material findings requiring decision review

### 1. Kayasandihai — `VENPA-USE-011`

The current proposal was designed to prevent Kayasandihai from disappearing after hunger relief. The primary epic already solves part of that problem: she speaks, gives Manimekalai useful direction and resumes her own course. The next review must decide whether to:

1. revise the proposal toward a compressed version of Kayasandihai's actual counsel and departure;
2. retain the current line as a deliberately non-specific adaptation;
3. reject the insertion because the screenplay cannot accommodate the source movement without expanding the branch.

Until that decision, `VENPA-USE-011` remains proposed but is **not approval-ready**.

### 2. Manimekalai's grief — `VENPA-USE-027`

The primary lament cannot be treated as a simple modern statement of grief without responsibility. It includes previous-life marital memory and language that may be read as assigning causal significance to disguise and convergence. The screenplay's existing architecture deliberately separates:

- previous cause from present permission;
- grief from consent;
- Manimekalai's disguise from Kanchanan's sword;
- emotional attachment from responsibility for Udayakumaran's repeated entry.

Any approved grief line must therefore be documented as a conscious ethical reinterpretation rather than a close translation.
