### Decision rationale

The earlier line about receiving news from roads was too passive and underused the primary epic's source-supported Kayasandihai movement. The selected revision preserves her as:

1. a body requiring water and rest;
2. a person whose recovery restores knowledge and speech;
3. a guide whose experience changes Manimekalai's understanding;
4. a character with a continuing course beyond hunger, disguise and Kanchanan's memory.

The wording is a functional compression, not direct primary dialogue. Because the screenplay already places Scene `#33#` at Ulaga Aravi, it cannot repeat the epic's literal geographical direction without breaking chronology.

- No insertion is not selected as the primary path, but remains a safety fallback if the revised line duplicates Scene `#32#` or fails spoken-Tamil review.

## VENPA-USE-027 — Manimekalai's grief

**Explicit decision:** `REVISE — SELECTED`  
**Selected option:** source-aware ethical reinterpretation  
**Performance fallback:** no additional line  
**Master operational status:** remains `PROPOSED`

### Selected English proposal

```text
MANIMEKALAI (CONT'D)
I grieve him. What I remember from another life does not make my answer in this one less true.
```

### Selected Tamil proposal

```text
மணிமேகலை (தொடர்ச்சி)
அவனை நினைத்து நான் துயரப்படுகிறேன். முந்தைய பிறவியின் நினைவு, இந்தப் பிறவியில் நான் சொன்ன மறுப்பின் உண்மையை குறைக்காது.
```

### Decision rationale

The selected revision acknowledges the previous-life attachment present in the primary lament rather than silently replacing it with wholly modern accountability language. It simultaneously preserves the feature's governing distinctions:

1. previous-life continuity is not present consent;
2. present refusal remains complete and truthful;
3. disguise is not the act that killed Udayakumaran;
4. Udayakumaran chose repeated entry after warning;
5. Kanchanan chose the sword;
6. grief does not create guilt, ownership or posthumous consent.

This line must be classified as `ADAPTED / DELIBERATE ETHICAL REINTERPRETATION`, not as a translation of Sattanar or Bharathidasan.

### Performance fallback

If the added line is too philosophical, repetitive or unnatural in immediate shock, retain the existing exchange without an additional sentence:

```text
MANIMEKALAI
I did not want him dead.

SUDHAMATHI
That does not make his pursuit your act.
```

No additional line is preferable to over-writing the grief beat.

### Earlier proposal superseded

The earlier wording `I will not call his refusal to hear me love` is no longer the preferred option. Its ethical meaning remains valid, but it is less source-aware and risks sounding argumentative at the moment of grief.
