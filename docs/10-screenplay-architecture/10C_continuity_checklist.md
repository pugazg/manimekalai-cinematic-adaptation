# 10C — Continuity and Draft-Readiness Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist controls the transition from the 72-unit beat architecture to the prose treatment. A unit is not treatment-ready merely because its dramatic function is attractive. It must preserve evidence, causation, knowledge order, physical continuity and the ethical safeguards already registered.

## A. Permanent identity and evidence

For every active unit:

- [ ] Preserve the `FU-*` or `BR-*` identifier from `10B_feature_unit_matrix.csv`.
- [ ] Preserve the primary `SC-*` identifier.
- [ ] Import all relevant evidence IDs from the primary and absorbed scene concepts.
- [ ] Import all binding adaptation decisions from the primary and absorbed concepts.
- [ ] Do not silently convert `[INTERPRETATION]` into `[TEXT]` or `[HISTORY]`.
- [ ] Record any newly invented connective action as interpretation.
- [ ] Do not create new permanent scene IDs during treatment drafting without updating the registers.

## B. Knowledge order

- [ ] Manimekalai's present refusal precedes any use of previous-life connection.
- [ ] Manipallavam revelation expands knowledge but does not give complete philosophical certainty.
- [ ] Aputhiran's story explains custodianship before public feeding begins.
- [ ] Aadhirai's first offering precedes the organised Ulaga Aravi expansion.
- [ ] Kanchanan acts from incomplete knowledge; identity correction follows the killing.
- [ ] Rajamadevi's coercive acts precede her recognition and relinquishment.
- [ ] Punniyarajan learns prior-life continuity before deciding whether to abandon or resume duty.
- [ ] Vanji comparative inquiry precedes Aravana's formal reasoning lesson.
- [ ] Chapter 30 causal teaching names patterns already dramatised.

## C. Causal continuity

- [ ] Chitrapathi's survival strategy may influence pursuit but never removes Udayakumaran's agency.
- [ ] Udayakumaran's sincere desire never functions as consent.
- [ ] Previous-life memory never functions as permission.
- [ ] Kanchanan's search and grief explain intensity but do not excuse concealment or killing.
- [ ] The fatal strike is preceded by an observable opportunity to question, withdraw or seek witnesses.
- [ ] Manimekalai's refusal is not framed as causing the prince's death.
- [ ] Rajamadevi's grief explains escalation but does not excuse coercion.
- [ ] Survival from drugging or starvation does not make the violence harmless.
- [ ] Punniyarajan's awakening does not release him automatically from living subjects.
- [ ] Famine is shown through interacting water, work, storage, price, access, health and governance failures.

## D. Branch-story entry and exit

For every `BR-*` unit:

- [ ] A present-tense speaker, object, question or place opens the branch.
- [ ] The branch answers a question active in the present scene.
- [ ] The branch has a clear visual and sonic grammar distinct from the present.
- [ ] The return point is prepared before the branch begins.
- [ ] The branch changes a present decision, relationship or responsibility.
- [ ] No branch continues merely because its historical material is interesting.
- [ ] The combined Aputhiran branch remains compressed within SEQ-04.
- [ ] The Aadhirai branch returns directly to the first offering.
- [ ] The Kanchanan branch returns directly to recognition and misinterpretation.

## E. Geography and travel

- [ ] Puhar remains a river-sea port shaped by labour, water and unequal abundance.
- [ ] Manipallavam remains an explicit cinematic hypothesis, not a falsely proven modern coordinate.
- [ ] Vanji remains a provisional Chera intellectual and exchange centre.
- [ ] Nāga identities are not assigned automatically to a modern ethnicity or nation.
- [ ] Sacred transport is not given a measurable scientific route unless the text supports it.
- [ ] Ordinary travel shows time, fatigue, supplies, weather, vehicles, labour and dependency.
- [ ] Punniyarajan's absence is negotiated and time-limited rather than administratively invisible.

## F. Time and runtime

- [ ] Sequence totals remain: 15, 15, 15, 20, 20, 20, 18, 15, 17 and 10 minutes.
- [ ] Total target remains 165 minutes.
- [ ] Branch beats use the runtime matrix as a ceiling, not an invitation to expand.
- [ ] SEQ-04 is drafted as intercut teaching and memory, not seventeen equally weighted scenes.
- [ ] SEQ-07 earns its 18 minutes through confrontation, relapse into possession, witnesses and relinquishment.
- [ ] SEQ-10 remains compressed and cumulative rather than becoming a second famine film.
- [ ] Any added scene requires a compensating cut and updated totals.

## G. Character continuity

### Manimekalai

- [ ] Fear, fatigue, grief and uncertainty remain visible inside compassion.
- [ ] Her artistic training remains embodied even after renunciation.
- [ ] She owns her testimony before teachers interpret it.
- [ ] She learns service logistics rather than operating as an effortless miracle worker.
- [ ] She does not become omniscient after Manipallavam.
- [ ] Her final stillness remains alert and socially responsive.

### Madhavi

- [ ] Renunciation does not erase artistic knowledge or household responsibility.
- [ ] Maternal protection does not become authorship of Manimekalai's future.
- [ ] Kovalan's death remains grief context, not moral contamination requiring purification.

### Sudhamathi

- [ ] She owns the telling of her earlier ordeal.
- [ ] Vigilance remains skilled perception, not comic fear or permanent trauma identity.
- [ ] Friendship permits disagreement and does not become surveillance.

### Udayakumaran

- [ ] Charisma and sincerity coexist with royal privilege and failure to hear refusal.
- [ ] The warning remains a real opportunity for correction.
- [ ] Death is not purification, romantic sacrifice or deserved punishment.

### Rajamadevi

- [ ] Formal imprisonment and private custody remain distinct.
- [ ] Drugging, defamation and starvation remain separate escalations.
- [ ] Apology or spiritual recognition does not erase accountability.
- [ ] Her completed action is relinquishment, not possession renamed as care.

### Aputhiran and Punniyarajan

- [ ] Same-actor casting remains unresolved unless separately approved.
- [ ] Previous-life continuity does not erase present identity.
- [ ] Compassion before miracle remains visible.
- [ ] Aputhiran's fast is sacred within the poem but not prescribed as universal self-erasure.
- [ ] Punniyarajan's return to rule is an active vow, not failed renunciation.

### Aadhirai

- [ ] The fire episode is not idealised or eroticised.
- [ ] Reunion does not erase Sāduvan's abandonment.
- [ ] Household giving shows skill, authority and material consequence.

### Kanchanan

- [ ] Kayasandihai remains a person, not a form guaranteeing ownership.
- [ ] Long search does not turn suspicion into proof.
- [ ] Concealment and weapon readiness remain choices.
- [ ] Revelation produces accountability, not automatic absolution.

## H. Amudhasurabhi and food continuity

- [ ] The vessel's physical design remains portable, dignified and materially believable.
- [ ] Its sacred effect is communicated through repeated service, not constant glow or spectacle.
- [ ] Custody is traceable from Aputhiran to Manipallavam to Manimekalai.
- [ ] Aadhirai's first offering occurs before large public distribution.
- [ ] Food service includes water, preparation, serving, cleaning, storage, access and crowd organisation.
- [ ] Recipients retain names, choices, relationships and bodily specificity.
- [ ] Disability and language access are built into service blocking.
- [ ] Human and nonhuman feeding do not create unsafe shared service spaces.
- [ ] No exact ancient recipe is claimed without philological and archaeobotanical review.

## I. Costume, body and disguise

- [ ] No modern Bharatanatyam fan costume or automatic medieval imperial Chola styling.
- [ ] Manimekalai's change is gradual, practical and dignified rather than colourless moral purification.
- [ ] No standard Buddhist robe colour or construction is locked without review.
- [ ] Kayasandihai's form carries social and marital risk, not only visual novelty.
- [ ] The sage form changes posture, voice, access and social reading; it is not comedy.
- [ ] Injuries, hunger, fatigue, imprisonment and travel alter movement consistently.

## J. Philosophy and dialogue

- [ ] No modern studio-panel debate staging.
- [ ] No Hindu-versus-Buddhist binary.
- [ ] Rival schools receive their strongest intelligible form.
- [ ] Personality is not treated as proof or disproof of doctrine.
- [ ] Private sacred experience is distinguished from public proof.
- [ ] Perception and inference are taught through a consequential error.
- [ ] Technical catalogues are replaced by dramatic examples without falsifying the argument.
- [ ] Dependent arising names a causal pattern already witnessed.
- [ ] Karma never becomes legal verdict or justification for deprivation.
- [ ] Liberation remains disciplined non-attachment with responsiveness to life.

## K. State and institutional continuity

- [ ] Festival promises establish a standard against which rule is measured.
- [ ] Prison reform changes water, kitchen, storage, lodging, access and roles—not merely set dressing.
- [ ] Palace coercion depends on attendants, orders, secrecy and reputation systems.
- [ ] Kanchi relief includes distribution, access, multilingual communication and governance accountability.
- [ ] Compassion is not confined to the protagonist; institutions and communities learn practices that can continue.

## L. Treatment gate

The 10D treatment may begin only when:

- [ ] all 72 active units have an objective, reversal and exit state;
- [ ] runtime totals equal 165 minutes;
- [ ] all branch entries and exits are specified;
- [ ] merged evidence and decisions are attached to surviving units;
- [ ] character-state progression contains no unexplained reversals;
- [ ] the Amudhasurabhi custody path is unbroken;
- [ ] geography and chronology uncertainties are labelled;
- [ ] no reserved or omitted scene has returned silently;
- [ ] the ending follows from the causal and philosophical progression rather than functioning as a sermon appended to the plot.
