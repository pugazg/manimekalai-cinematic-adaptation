# 10B — Scene Selection and Consolidation Matrix

## Feature Architecture Edition 0.1

**Project:** *Manimekalai* cinematic adaptation  
**Feature target:** 165 minutes  
**Input:** 154 registered screenplay scene concepts, `SC-001` through `SC-154`  
**Output:** 61 principal feature scenes, 11 embedded branch beats, 69 merged concepts, 10 reserve concepts and 3 omissions

---

## 1. Purpose

The screenplay evidence matrix is a research archive, not a literal shooting script.

Many registered scenes were created at different stages to protect a particular historical, ethical, philosophical, character or production-design function. Several therefore describe the same dramatic event from different research angles.

The purpose of 10B is to:

- retain every important evidence function;
- prevent repeated versions of the same event;
- protect Manimekalai's protagonist spine;
- limit embedded stories to the time needed to change a present decision;
- preserve the ten-sequence, 165-minute architecture;
- and make every compression decision reversible and auditable.

No permanent `SC-*` identifier is deleted or renumbered.

---

## 2. Selection vocabulary

### KEEP

The scene becomes the **primary surviving scene concept** for a feature-film beat.

Its final screenplay card must inherit relevant evidence and adaptation decisions from all concepts merged into it.

### MERGE

The scene does not remain a separate screenplay beat.

Its evidence, visual detail, character purpose or institutional function is absorbed into a named `KEEP` or `BRANCH` scene.

### BRANCH

The scene remains as a compressed embedded-story beat.

A branch scene must:

- answer a present-tense question;
- change a present choice or understanding;
- have a clear entry and exit point;
- and avoid creating a second competing feature film.

### RESERVE

The concept is valuable but is not required for the working 165-minute cut.

It may return in:

- an extended cut;
- a series adaptation;
- a visual transition;
- a background action layer;
- or a later continuity repair.

### OMIT

The concept is removed as a standalone feature beat because it opens a material, dynastic or economic detour that does not sufficiently advance the protagonist, causal tragedy, hunger ethic or philosophical climax.

The underlying research remains in the repository.

- harbour labour;
- water and sanitation work;
- festival preparation;

- exclusion;

## 8. Binding drafting rules after consolidation

1. A merged scene retains all relevant evidence IDs and binding adaptation decisions from the absorbed concepts.
2. A `MERGE` label does not authorise deleting the ethical or historical function that caused the original scene to be registered.
3. Branch stories must be entered through a present character's question, testimony, object or decision.
4. Branch exits must return with changed understanding or responsibility.
5. Reserve scenes cannot be restored casually; their runtime and causal consequences must be recalculated.
6. Omitted scenes remain valid research references but cannot be cited as events occurring in the screenplay.
7. The 165-minute runtime remains a ceiling for the first treatment.
8. Philosophy must continue through action, inference and consequence rather than speech volume.
9. Manimekalai must remain the active centre even when another character temporarily carries the image track.
10. Scene lengths will be assigned in 10C; the `major`, `standard` and `embedded` labels in the unit matrix are relative weights, not final minute counts.
