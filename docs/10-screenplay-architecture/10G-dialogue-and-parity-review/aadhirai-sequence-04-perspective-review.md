## Source-perspective findings

The Venpa gives the Aadhirai movement a clear dramatic order:

1. Aadhirai sees Manimekalai with the vessel and welcomes her with joy.
2. Aadhirai gives cooked rice with curry; the verse also evokes ghee flowing with the food.
3. The vessel is filled by an ordinary household act of generosity before its inexhaustibility becomes socially visible.
4. People see or hear of the offering, gather and praise it.
5. Manimekalai then invites everyone to eat, while the vessel continues to remain full.

Relevant OCR headings include:

- `ஆதிரை வரவேற்பு`
- `ஆதிரை கறியோடு சோறிட்டாள்`
- `ஆதிரை வாழ்த்து`
- `ஆதிரை இட்ட அமிழ்து`
- `மணிமேகலை எல்லார்க்கும் இட்டாள்`

The key OCR line is:

`கலமே நிறையக் கறியொடு சோறிட்டாள்`

Exact quotation and dialogue use remain subject to verification against a reliable page image or printed edition.

## Executive finding

The existing screenplay already understands Aadhirai's importance. She is not a cameo. Scenes `#29#`–`#31#` give her:

- survival after society prematurely treats her life as finished;
- moral independence within reunion;
- authority to judge whether Sāduvan's claimed change becomes lived change;
- control of the household threshold and stores;
- the first act that turns Amudhasurabhi from possession into public trust.

The scene's present lines about food behind one door and crossing doors closed to hunger are strong and should be retained.

The principal gap is visual and causal: the screenplay currently gives Aadhirai a **first handful of grain**, while the Venpa gives her a **substantial prepared meal that fills the vessel**. A handful makes the human gift symbolic and leaves most of the food to miracle. The Venpa's order is stronger: human labour and generosity fill the bowl first; inexhaustibility is revealed only when that food is shared and does not run out.

## Scene findings

### Scene #29 — BR-010 / SC-150 / ABSORBS SC-043, SC-044, SC-148, SC-149

The screenplay refuses to present Aadhirai's threatened death by fire as proof of ideal wifehood. It centres her continued life after rumour and social expectation have declared that life finished.

**Finding:** Strong and ethically controlled.  
**Recommended change:** None.  
**Safeguard:** Do not add spectacle that recentres the fire rather than Aadhirai's survival.

### Scene #30 — BR-011 / SC-152 / ABSORBS SC-151

Aadhirai does not reward Sāduvan's return with immediate emotional restoration. Her line, `Change must be lived after arrival`, gives her moral authority and prevents reunion from erasing abandonment, rumour or the life she maintained alone.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended structural change:** None.  
**Later P2/P4 flag:** The Tamil rendering is literary but clear; test its spoken rhythm before dialogue lock.

### Scene #31 — FU-021 / SC-153 / ABSORBS SC-017, SC-154

The threshold, first offering and absence of a witnessing crowd create an intimate beginning. Aadhirai gives trust rather than tribute. The door imagery links private stores to public hunger.

**Finding:** Thematic structure is excellent; food action is under-scaled relative to the Venpa.  
**Recommended approach:** Preserve the scene, lines and trace. Replace the handful of uncooked grain with prepared household food that visibly fills the vessel by human action.

### Scene #32 — FU-022 / SC-068 / ABSORBS SC-006, SC-018

The public-feeding scene correctly shows that the vessel alone is insufficient: water, queues, translation, cleaning, access and shared labour are required.

**Finding:** Strong systems transition.  
**Opportunity:** Reveal inexhaustibility only after ordinary portions have been served from Aadhirai's food and the level does not fall.

### VENPA-USE-009

**Element:** Move the whole public crowd to Aadhirai's threshold so townspeople witness and praise the first offering exactly as in the Venpa.  
**Use type:** `DIRECT`  
**Status:** `REJECTED`

**Reason:** The existing quiet threshold gives the first act moral intimacy. Scene `#32#` already supplies public gathering and collective response. Moving the crowd into Scene `#31#` would duplicate function, weaken the threshold image and make Aadhirai's gift performative.

The existing transition — `The wish travels beyond the threshold` followed by a crowd forming rapidly — is an effective cinematic adaptation of news and praise spreading through the town.

## Bilingual and terminology observations

These are P2/P3/P4 flags only; they are not approved changes:

1. English uses `AADHIRAI`; the Tamil screenplay uses `ஆதிரை`. Other supplied sources use forms such as Adirai, Ādirai and Āthirai. Choose one transliteration policy only during terminology review.
2. The Tamil phrase `மாற்றம் வந்த பின்பும் வாழ்ந்து காட்டப்பட வேண்டும்` preserves the intended moral demand but should be tested aloud.
3. `அந்த அளிப்பை மரியாதையாக அல்ல — நம்பிக்கையாகப் பெறுகிறாள்` strongly preserves `trust, not tribute` and should remain.
4. If `VENPA-USE-007` is approved, `கறி` should be understood in its food context and not modernly narrowed without culinary review.
5. English and Tamil must preserve identical Scene `#31#` and `#32#` trace metadata.
