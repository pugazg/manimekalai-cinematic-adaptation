# Tamil Sequence 03 Semantic Review

## Scope

This review compares English Sequence 03, Scenes `#14#–#17#`, with the Tamil performance adaptation. It tests semantic, causal and ethical parity. It does not add a new source perspective, alter scene structure or approve dialogue lock.

## Result

The Tamil sequence preserved the four scenes and their TRACE signatures, but compressed several beats that carry character vulnerability, epistemic discipline and the distinction between memory and present consent. One sentence in Scene `#14#` also reversed the intended relationship between sacred transport and physical vulnerability.

The Tamil sequence has been revised to restore the necessary meanings while retaining concise, performable action writing.

## Scene `#14#` — Physical and observational continuity

Restored:

- Manimekalai waiting for dizziness to pass rather than appearing instantly composed;
- testing wet sand, reading birds and measuring the sun;
- discovering that only her footprints are present;
- moving inland while both afraid and attentive;
- the distinction that sacred transport removed familiar protections but did not make her invulnerable;
- the sound of water leading toward the grove.

These are not decorative details. They establish that Manimekalai responds to the supernatural through embodied observation rather than instant certainty.

## Scene `#15#` — Memory, identity and public proof

Restored:

- Manimekalai's initial mistrust of Deepathilahai;
- Udayakumaran's remembered face separating from present identity;
- revelation enlarging identity without surrendering it;
- physical contact with the sacred seat;
- the distinction between a sacred experience that is real to Manimekalai and evidence she may compel others to accept.

The existing consent safeguard remains unchanged: memory cannot create consent for a living person.

## Scene `#16#` — Obligation rather than possession

Revised and restored:

- the contrast between sharing abundance and claiming it;
- the unknown lives that may depend on Manimekalai's next action;
- her physical embrace of the vessel;
- the conclusion that accepting the vessel begins an obligation rather than completing one.

The Amudhasurabhi remains physically light and ethically heavy. It is not treated as treasure or proof of completed spiritual authority.

## Scene `#17#` — Honest testimony and return

Restored:

- Sudhamathi's observation that the night changed around Manimekalai;
- her refusal to invent certainty for Madhavi;
- the two facts she can responsibly affirm: Manimekalai did not leave by choice and was alive when last seen;
- the dramatic value of testimony that states its own limits;
- the city's prior reduction of Manimekalai to Madhavi's daughter and the object of a prince's desire;
- the responsibility she carries back to a city not yet able to understand it.

## Controls preserved

- English screenplay text: unchanged.
- Scene numbers: unchanged.
- TRACE signatures: unchanged.
- Previous-life continuity: not present consent.
- Sacred experience: not automatic public proof.
- Manimekalai: physically vulnerable and intellectually unfinished.
- Sudhamathi: credible because she distinguishes observation from inference.
- New Venpa material: none.

## Gate status

Internal semantic comparison is complete. Sequence 03 still requires specialist terminology review, character-voice review and an actor-led read-aloud timing pass before dialogue lock.
