# Tamil Sequence 07 Spoken-Performance Review

## Scope and result

This internal pass compares English and Tamil Sequence 07, Scenes `#50#–#53#`. The sequence preserved the required movement from confrontation through possessive care, public evidence and unconditional release. The revised Tamil clarifies grief, guilt, responsibility, repair and ownership without making Rajamadevi's transformation easier or more complete than the English master.

## Findings

- Grief remains pain; actions ordered through grief may become crimes.
- Manimekalai separates refusal, inference, choice and command while accepting only what belongs to her.
- She mourns Udayakumaran without converting persistent desire into mutual love.
- Gratitude can hold a person in a room just as fear can; every new label for custody is tested against whether she may leave.
- Entry at the palace gate rests on testimony and preservable evidence, not invented spiritual authority.
- Madhavi asks before approaching; Sudhamathi supports only after consent.
- Chitrapathi names her error without asking Manimekalai to relieve her guilt.
- Rajamadevi publicly traces the cup, accusation and withheld food back to her own power.
- Permission becomes unconditional release only when the Queen moves away from the threshold.
- Manimekalai hears the first accountable act but does not remain to certify redemption.

## Controls preserved

- English master, scene numbers and TRACE: unchanged.
- Recognition: not absolution.
- Care: tested for possession.
- Release: unconditional and bodily visible.
- Manimekalai: not required to comfort or validate Rajamadevi.
- Venpa material: none added.
