## Executive finding

The present screenplay already gives Madhavi substantially more dramatic depth than the earlier perspective audit assumed. Sequence 01 does not reduce her to exposition. It gives her:

- grief that is not treated as a completed event;
- responsibility toward workers and household dependants;
- awareness of the economic and personal cost of the performance institution;
- fear for her daughter without claiming ownership over her future;
- the discipline to distinguish concern from pursuit;
- a clear relationship to Kovalan and Kannagi without making Manimekalai inherit their obligations.

A broad Madhavi expansion is therefore **not recommended** for Sequences 01–02. The only high-value gap is Scene `#13#`, where Madhavi discovers the empty room but is not yet given an active ethical choice.

## Scene findings

### Scene #3 — FU-003 / SC-073

Madhavi's house is shown as an economic institution, not merely a private emotional space. Madhavi refuses to compel anyone to remain and accepts responsibility for wages and assets. This already expresses active renunciation rather than escape.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended structural change:** None.  
**Risk of expansion:** Additional explanation could weaken the scene's balance between Madhavi, Chitrapathi, the workers and Manimekalai.

### Scene #4 — BR-001 / SC-097

This is the strongest Madhavi scene in the reviewed block. Her grief over Kovalan, the destruction associated with Kannagi, public judgement and maternal responsibility are all present. Aravana's response explicitly protects Manimekalai as a life beside Madhavi's life rather than an answer to Madhavi's grief.

**Finding:** Strong and perspective-compatible.  
**Recommended narrative change:** None.  
**Later P2 flag:** The Tamil line `அது பதில் அல்ல. உன் வாழ்வின் அருகில் இருக்கும் இன்னொரு வாழ்வு.` is accurate but deliberately literary. It should be tested aloud before dialogue lock; no wording change is approved here.

### Scene #5 — FU-004 / SC-075

The mother–daughter conflict is already nuanced. Madhavi explains what the anklet cost her, but Manimekalai is allowed to reject inherited interpretation. Madhavi asks what exists beyond refusal rather than prescribing a destination. Her fear is visible, but the scene does not convert fear into control.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended structural change:** None.  
**Deferred possibility:** A direct line stating that Madhavi's fear must not name Manimekalai's life would repeat what the scene already communicates through action and restraint.

### Scene #6 — FU-005 / SC-009

Madhavi remains behind while Chitrapathi searches. The action line makes the ethical choice explicit: concern is not turned into pursuit.

**Finding:** Strong visual characterization.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #13 — FU-012 / SC-085 / ABSORBS SC-076

The empty-room ending preserves uncertainty but gives Madhavi no active response. Because Scene #6 has already shown her refusal to turn concern into pursuit, Scene #13 is the best location for one small action that confirms how she responds under greater fear.

**Finding:** Under-developed relative to the strength of Sequence 01.  
**Recommended approach:** Strengthen the existing scene; do not add a new scene or TRACE unit.

4. The Tamil rendering of Madhavi remaining behind in Scene #6 preserves the distinction between care and pursuit.
5. Any accepted Scene #13 addition must be made in English and Tamil together and must retain identical trace metadata.
