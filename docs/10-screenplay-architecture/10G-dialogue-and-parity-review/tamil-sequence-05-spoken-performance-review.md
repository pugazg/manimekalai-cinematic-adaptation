# Tamil Sequence 05 Spoken-Performance Review

## Scope

This internal pass compares English and Tamil Sequence 05, Scenes `#35#–#43#`, and tests the Tamil for speakability, semantic precision, character voice and ethical causality. It is not an actor table read and does not grant final dialogue lock.

## Result

The restored Tamil sequence preserved every scene, action outcome and TRACE signature. The principal problems were translation-shaped sentence order, abstract noun clusters and several phrases that weakened the distinction between affection, refusal, authority, inference and action.

The Tamil has been tightened without adding plot or changing the English master.

## Character and scene findings

### Chitrapathi and Udayakumaran — Scenes `#35#–#36#`

- Chitrapathi's language now distinguishes royal support from romantic attention.
- Her fear remains material: workers, household survival and the dangers surrounding Manimekalai.
- Udayakumaran's sincerity remains audible, but double-negative phrasing no longer softens his decision to convert remembered cause into present expectation.
- Manimekalai's answer is now more direct in spoken Tamil: affection that will not accept refusal may become violence.

### Kanchanan's search and mistaken recognition — Scenes `#37#–#39#`

- The montage now names both the reality of his search and the expectation of a rewarded reunion.
- Manimekalai's refusal is expressed as a person speaking about an incorrectly named woman, not through clinical phrasing.
- Uncertainty remains uncertainty. Kanchanan supplies concealment and betrayal from his own fear.
- The action language now makes his perceptual error clearer: physical distance resembles intimacy, concealment resembles betrayal and pursuit resembles invitation.

### Preparation and killing — Scenes `#40#–#42#`

- Suspicion is explicitly converted into permission to act.
- Udayakumaran no longer claims literally to have abandoned rank; he arrives without its visible signs while still carrying its power.
- The killing remains planned, brief and without heroic framing.
- Sacred correction explains causation without moving the sword or granting absolution.

### Aftermath — Scene `#43#`

- Sudhamathi's language continues to separate Manimekalai's grief from responsibility for pursuit.
- Kanchanan's grief explains the road but not the hand.
- Manimekalai asks that testimony be heard before grief becomes judgement; the Tamil now uses a performable legal register without changing the institutional meaning.

## Controls preserved

- English screenplay: unchanged.
- Scene numbers and TRACE signatures: unchanged.
- Previous-life memory: cause, not permission.
- Refusal: complete and present-tense.
- Disguise: not causal guilt.
- Kanchanan: understandable grief, accountable act.
- Udayakumaran: sincere feeling, persistent entitlement.
- Venpa material: none added.
