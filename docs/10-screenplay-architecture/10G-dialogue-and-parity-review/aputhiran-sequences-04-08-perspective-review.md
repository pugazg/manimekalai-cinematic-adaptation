## Source-perspective findings

The Venpa presents Aputhiran as more than the earlier owner of a miraculous vessel. Its emphasis moves through a connected ethical sequence:

1. Aravana tells Manimekalai to remove the disease of hunger among people and learn what dharma means through that work.
2. Aputhiran's fame is tied to moral resistance, including opposition to sacrificial killing.
3. He does not weaken when confronted by hostility.
4. Reactionary opposition attempts to obstruct or destroy a person acting through compassion.
5. After hearing his history, Manimekalai carries the vessel into public life rather than keeping it as a sacred possession.
6. Giving becomes collective: people who have contributed food return to the place where the vessel is kept and contribute again.
7. The Venpa's imagery eventually makes the act of giving, the vessel and the giver part of one shared social function.

Relevant OCR headings and lines include:

- `ஆபுத்திரன் பற்றி அறவண அடிகள்`

## Executive finding

The present screenplay already gives Aputhiran a complete and unusually disciplined ethical arc. It neither treats him as a saint whose every action must be imitated nor reduces him to a cautionary failure.

The screenplay already establishes that:

- care precedes doctrine, ancestry and recognition;
- opposition to animal sacrifice is a question of the life being harmed, not ritual prestige;
- Aputhiran feeds strangers with ordinary food before receiving a miraculous vessel;
- his refusal of heavenly reward is courageous;
- courage can coexist with pride and a need to remain indispensable;
- the end of famine is good even when it removes his familiar role;
- service depends on sailors, workers, routes and collective labour;
- his final fast is tragic rather than triumphant;
- the vessel must reach a hand that can serve without owning;
- Manimekalai must let hungry people teach her what service requires;
- compassion must become logistics and institutional change;
- Punniyarajan may inherit memory or capacity without becoming Aputhiran;
- public systems are not ethically inferior merely because no single giver receives praise;
- remembrance is completed through administrative reform, not disappearance or memorial ritual.

A broad rewrite is therefore **not recommended**.

Two narrow opportunities remain:

1. Aputhiran's rejection of Indra's reward is currently expressed mainly through dialogue. One visible act of immediately returning to a hungry person would make the refusal materially complete.
2. Punniyarajan's reforms are strong, but they are still issued from the king's voice. One additional instruction can ensure that relief does not depend on one ruler, one seal or one heroic life.

## Scene findings

### Scene #20 — FU-019 / SC-100

The ordinary begging bowl is placed beside Amudhasurabhi, and Aravana instructs the audience not to begin with miracle but with hunger.

**Finding:** Essential governing scene.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #21 — BR-002 / SC-120

The abandoned infant is kept alive by a cow while human beings fail to notice. Care precedes doctrine and social recognition.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #22 — BR-003 / SC-121

Aputhiran reads the restrained cow's body before listening to ritual explanation. His statement that the animal does not consent to another person's holiness gives the anti-sacrifice movement a direct ethical centre.

**Finding:** Strong and Venpa-compatible.  
**Recommended change:** None.  
**Safeguard:** Do not turn the scene into a general attack on a community when its dramatic subject is the life being harmed and the social cost of resistance.

### Scene #23 — BR-004 / SC-123 / ABSORBS SC-122

Aputhiran gathers ordinary food, serves people whose mobility and access differ, feeds a child who shares with an animal and eats only after others.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended change:** None.  
**Key strength:** Compassion exists as practice before miracle, fame or divine recognition.

### Scene #24 — BR-005 / SC-124

Aputhiran rejects Indra's offer by demanding food for those heaven has left hungry. The scene then identifies both courage and the first flicker of pride.

**Finding:** Conceptually strong; the refusal remains mostly verbal.  
**Recommended approach:** Add one silent service action after the refusal and before the interpretive narration.

### Scene #25 — BR-006 / SC-125 / ABSORBS SC-016

Rain ends the famine, and the empty feeding hall is correctly named as good news. Aputhiran's inability to understand service without dependence begins the screenplay's critique of indispensability.

**Finding:** Strong adaptation choice.  
**Recommended change:** None.  
**Safeguard:** Do not portray people who no longer need relief as ungrateful merely because Aputhiran has lost a role.

### Scenes #26–#27 — BR-007 / SC-127 and BR-008 / SC-128

The voyage depends on many hands; the separation is not caused by a simple villain. Aputhiran releases the vessel for a future custodian, while his final fast remains tragic and distant.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended change:** None.  
**Safeguard:** Do not convert self-erasure into the ethical climax of his life.

### Scene #28 — FU-020 / SC-101 / ABSORBS SC-077

Manimekalai identifies the danger of being unable to imagine oneself without giving. Aravana separates compassion from the wish to be indispensable and sends the vessel into public accountability.

**Finding:** The ethical hinge of the entire Aputhiran movement.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #32 — FU-022 / SC-068 / ABSORBS SC-006, SC-018

Recipients, water carriers, cleaners, translators and other participants construct access around the vessel. The screenplay already embodies the Venpa's movement from one giver toward collective giving.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended change:** None.  
**Key strength:** `The vessel is food. The work is everyone reaching it safely.` prevents miracle from replacing organisation.

### Scene #34 — FU-024 / SC-020 / ABSORBS SC-037, SC-051

A former prisoner receives the first ladle because lived knowledge of the institution matters. Food is supplied by the vessel, but people reorganise power.

**Finding:** Strong institutional continuation.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scenes #54–#58 — FU-044 through FU-048

Punniyarajan's prosperity is shown as maintained infrastructure rather than royal magic. He delegates before travelling, learns that rebirth does not merge identities, confronts collateral consequences and rejects disappearance as an imitation of Aputhiran.

**Finding:** Exceptionally strong continuity architecture.  
**Recommended structural change:** None.

### Scene #59 — FU-049 / SC-146 / ABSORBS SC-147

Punniyarajan returns and translates memory into canal rules, inspectable grain records and routes for people unable to travel. Administration becomes the memorial.

**Finding:** Strong culmination.  
**Opportunity:** Make the system explicitly capable of acting when the king or palace cannot be reached, completing the movement away from indispensability.
