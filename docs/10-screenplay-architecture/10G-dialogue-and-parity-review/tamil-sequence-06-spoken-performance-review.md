# Tamil Sequence 06 Spoken-Performance Review

## Scope

This internal pass compares English and Tamil Sequence 06, Scenes `#44#–#49#`. It tests speakability, semantic precision, character voice and the visibility of delegated state harm. It is not an actor table read and does not grant dialogue lock.

## Result

The Tamil restoration preserved structure and causality. Several literal constructions nevertheless obscured who chose, ordered, carried, recorded or concealed each action. The revised Tamil makes those relationships easier to perform and hear without adding any new offence, witness or institutional mechanism.

## Scene findings

### Hearing and mourning — Scenes `#44#–#45#`

- Testimony is now consistently distinguished from belief, grief and accusation.
- Manimekalai's line names two present choices: Udayakumaran crossed her refusal; Kanchanan trusted his own inference.
- The king's restraint remains the minimum duty of rule rather than emotional delay.
- Vasantavai now describes persistent pursuit without the positive connotations carried by a literal word for perseverance.
- Gentleness does not make refusal temporary.

### Concealed custody — Scene `#46#`

- Rajamadevi's public language of protection remains distinct from the locked private room.
- Manimekalai's constrained entry is expressed directly: a public refusal would be converted into evidence of guilt.
- Vasantavai's contrast between motherhood and queenship is now more speakable and more exact about command.

### Poisoning and evidence — Scene `#47#`

- The young attendant remains a coerced carrier rather than the originator of the act.
- The older servant's intervention preserves the cup, the witnesses and the chain from instruction to locked room.
- Tamil syntax now keeps evidence and witness relationships audible during performance.

### Fabricated accusation — Scene `#48#`

- The indebted man remains pressured, accountable and eventually unwilling to become another person's lie.
- Manimekalai tests embodied knowledge rather than reputation.
- The rehearsed story now clearly lacks the memory of a person who was physically present.
- Formal failure does not prevent public rumour from becoming social power.

### Starvation system — Scene `#49#`

- Deprivation is named as food and water being withheld through routine obedience.
- False delivery, false refusal and false spiritual-fasting records remain distinct.
- Manimekalai controls her breathing but does not cease to have a hungry body.
- Separate pieces of evidence accumulate until Rajamadevi must see the system created by her command.
- The final challenge now asks whether the Queen has come to grieve or to demand an account from another body.
