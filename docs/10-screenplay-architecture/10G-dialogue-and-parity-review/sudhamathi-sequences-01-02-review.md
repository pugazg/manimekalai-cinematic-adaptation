## Executive finding

Sudhamathi is already a major dramatic intelligence in Sequences 01–02. She is not merely an expositional companion. The screenplay gives her four distinct functions:

- she protects Manimekalai without replacing Manimekalai's voice;
- she reads streets, witnesses and authority through lived experience;
- she converts personal survival into practical knowledge;
- she remains the truthful continuity witness when Manimekalai disappears.

A large expansion is not required. The main opportunity is Scene `#8#`, where her personal history is described in action prose but her practical method remains mostly abstract. One concrete line can make her strategy visible without staging or detailing violence.

## Scene findings

### Scene #3 — FU-003 / SC-073

Sudhamathi listens while folding cloth. This is a quiet introduction rather than a passive definition. She is positioned inside the household's labour and conflict before she speaks.

**Finding:** Adequate setup.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #5 — FU-004 / SC-075

Sudhamathi watches Manimekalai and remains ready to intervene without speaking for her. Moving the folded robe away from all three objects is consequential: she interrupts a false forced choice and creates time for Manimekalai.

Her line that Manimekalai can walk before naming the road gives her a distinct ethical method. She protects process rather than choosing the destination.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended change:** None.  
**Safeguard:** Do not add dialogue that makes Sudhamathi decide Manimekalai's vocation.

### Scene #6 — FU-005 / SC-009

Sudhamathi combines philosophical observation with route planning. She reads both arguments and streets. The final image preserves Madhavi's restraint while Sudhamathi takes responsibility for the safer route.

**Finding:** Strong continuity function.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #7 — FU-006 / SC-081

The screenplay establishes her practical street intelligence through doorways, mounted officers and ignored warning signs. The image of a crowd watching a young woman being pulled aside, followed by Sudhamathi continuing to walk, is deliberately disturbing.

**Finding:** Powerful but vulnerable to misreading.  
**Risk:** Without Scene #8 clarifying her method, an audience may read continued movement as indifference rather than survival-trained assessment.  
**Recommended approach:** Clarify her method in Scene #8 rather than adding an intervention that changes the event or makes her responsible for policing every act of violence.

### Scene #8 — FU-007 / SC-082 / ABSORBS SC-056

The scene correctly refuses to turn Sudhamathi's suffering into spectacle. However, its current action prose says that she passes practical knowledge to Manimekalai while the only spoken line remains broad and civic.

**Finding:** Thematic strength; insufficient concrete method.  
**Recommended approach:** Add one short line before the existing statement about whose voice a city permits.

### Scene #9 — FU-008 / SC-055 / ABSORBS SC-005, SC-083, SC-089

`The prince sees beauty. Sudhamathi sees exposure.` gives Sudhamathi a sharp point of view. The contrast efficiently frames royal arrival as risk rather than romance.

**Finding:** Strong.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #10 — FU-009 / SC-058 / ABSORBS SC-049, SC-065, SC-084, SC-091

Manimekalai gives her own refusal. Sudhamathi does not take over the confrontation. This correctly preserves the distinction between protection and substitution.

**Finding:** Strong agency balance.  
**Recommended change:** None.

### Scene #13 — FU-012 / SC-085 / ABSORBS SC-076

Sudhamathi cannot explain everything but can state what she witnessed. This makes her the bridge between disappearance, truth and household response. The proposed Madhavi beat in `VENPA-USE-001` would strengthen this function by explicitly asking for witnessed truth rather than public assumption.

**Finding:** Strong premise, brief execution.  
**Recommended change:** Do not add a second Sudhamathi speech until `VENPA-USE-001` is decided; the two proposals must not compete.
