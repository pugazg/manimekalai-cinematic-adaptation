# Tamil Screenplay Adaptation Guidelines

## 1. Governing principle

The Tamil screenplay is a cinematic adaptation of the approved English working screenplay. It is not a line-by-line translation of the source epic and it is not a licence to change the approved dramatic architecture.

Preserve meaning, action, causation, consent and responsibility before preserving English syntax.

## 2. Register

Use clear, dignified, performable Tamil.

- Avoid modern Chennai slang unless a later production pass explicitly chooses it.
- Avoid making every character speak in heavily literary Tamil.
- Avoid pseudo-classical ornament inserted only to sound ancient.
- Prefer sentences an actor can speak while pursuing an objective.
- Keep philosophical dialogue precise but emotionally situated.

## 3. Character voices

### மணிமேகலை

Direct, observant and increasingly disciplined. She does not speak like an omniscient teacher in Act I. Her language moves from questions and refusal toward responsible clarity.

### மாதவி

Controlled, emotionally compressed and conscious of social consequence. Her grief should not become decorative poetry.

### சுதமதி

Practical, survivor-informed and alert to bodily danger. She speaks concretely and does not romanticise suffering.

### சித்திராபதி

Strategic, economically literate and unsentimental. She may be wrong without becoming a caricature.

### உதயகுமரன்

Courteous and sincere, but unable to recognise how power survives inside courtesy. His Tamil must not make him an obvious villain from his first line.

### அறவண அடிகள்

Patient and exact. He teaches through questions, distinctions and consequences rather than long sermons.

### இராசமாதேவி

Royal control strained by grief. Her speech moves from command and euphemism toward public accountability.

## 5. Consent and agency safeguards

Tamil adaptation must retain the following distinctions exactly:

- familiarity is not consent;
- previous-life connection is not present ownership;
- gentleness does not cancel power;
- grief may explain action but does not transfer responsibility;
- protection without freedom is custody;
- apology does not create a claim over the harmed person;
- compassion does not require indispensability.

## 6. Philosophical vocabulary

Where an exact doctrinal Tamil term is uncertain, use clear conceptual Tamil and flag the line in a non-printing note:

`/* TAMIL TERMINOLOGY REVIEW: ... */`

Do not invent technical terminology merely to create an ancient tone.

## 7. Fountain formatting

- Preserve English-style `INT.`, `EXT.` and `INT./EXT.` scene prefixes for compatibility.
- Translate locations and time-of-day descriptions into Tamil.
- Preserve scene numbers such as `#14#`.
- Preserve trace comments unchanged.
- Character cues are in Tamil script.
- Parentheticals should be rare and performable.

## 8. Adaptation latitude

Permitted:

- changing word order;
- splitting a long sentence;
- replacing an English idiom with an equivalent Tamil dramatic line;
- using repetition where Tamil performance benefits;
- shortening exposition already communicated visually.

Not permitted:

- adding new plot facts;
- changing who knows what;
- making a refusal ambiguous;
- converting institutional harm into one villain's private act;
- changing the outcome or ethical meaning of a scene;
- introducing unsupported ritual, caste, costume or archaeological certainty.

## 9. Quality gate

For every sequence, compare English and Tamil side by side and verify:

1. every scene is present;
2. every action with causal importance is present;
3. every refusal remains unambiguous;
4. every trace ID matches;
5. no added line changes historical or doctrinal claims;
6. dialogue sounds speakable aloud;
7. character voices remain distinguishable.
