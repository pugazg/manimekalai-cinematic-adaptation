## Governing rules

- Philosophy begins before Chapter 27 through desire, refusal, memory, hunger, grief and mistaken inference.
- A named position is first evidence for how this Buddhist epic represents it; it is not automatically a complete neutral historical creed.
- Rival teachers receive their strongest intelligible argument and individual dramatic dignity.
- Chapter 27 is comparative inquiry, Chapter 29 is disciplined reasoning and Chapter 30 is causal and ethical practice.
- Manimekalai may possess true experience within the epic's world while still learning that private experience is not automatically public proof.
- No doctrine is retained in the feature merely to complete a catalogue; every encounter must change her question.
- Buddhist commitment remains explicit without converting the sequence into propaganda or a modern interfaith panel.
- Karma explains continuity within the story world but never functions as legal verdict, socioeconomic victim-blaming or permission to abandon present responsibility.
- Liberation is not emotional numbness, social disappearance or self-erasure.
