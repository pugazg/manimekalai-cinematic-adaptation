# 05A — Philosophical Map of Manimekalai

## Philosophy Bible Edition 0.1

## Purpose

This section translates the epic's philosophical material into cinematic questions without reducing it to lectures or modern categories.

The film must preserve three layers:

1. ideas about reality;
2. methods of knowing;
3. ethical consequences in lived life.

---

## Central philosophical questions

### 1. What causes suffering?

The epic repeatedly returns to:

- hunger;
- desire;
- attachment;
- ignorance;
- repeated existence;
- social failure.

These are not separate topics. They appear through characters.

---

### 2. How do humans know truth?

Manimekalai does not accept teachings merely because they are powerful.

She asks:

- How do you know?
- What evidence supports this?
- Does the teaching explain experience?
- Does it reduce suffering?

---

### 3. What is liberation?

Liberation is not escape from responsibility.

The screenplay must show the difference between:

- abandoning the world;
- understanding attachment;
- serving without possession.

---

## Philosophy entering drama

Every philosophical idea must change a choice.

Examples:

- Hunger philosophy changes Manimekalai's action.
- Rebirth changes Punniyarajan's understanding of kingship.
- Impermanence changes Udayakumaran's tragedy.
- Attachment changes Kanchanan's failure.

---

## Core rule

Philosophy is not background information in *Manimekalai*.

It is the reason characters choose, suffer, transform and act.
