# PART I -- PUHAR

## Chapter 1 -- Indra Festival

**Literary Function:** Introduces Puhar as a prosperous cosmopolitan capital and establishes the cultural world of the story.

**Dramatic Function:** Creates scale before introducing the heroine.

**Emotional Function:** Wonder.

**Cinematic Opportunity:** One of the largest sequences in the film: ships, markets, elephants, music, dancers and foreign merchants.

**Decision:** Expand substantially. This becomes the opening act rather than a short introduction.

---

## Chapter 2 -- Gossip of the City

**Literary Function:** Explains Madhavi's renunciation and Manimekalai's lineage.

**Dramatic Function:** Introduces inherited trauma.

**Decision:** Intercut with memories of Kovalan and Kannagi rather than relying on exposition.

---

## Chapter 3 -- Flower Garden

**Literary Function:** Introduces Manimekalai and Sudhamathi.

**Dramatic Function:** Begins the central emotional journey.

**Decision:** Preserve almost completely. The journey through Puhar becomes an opportunity to reveal the city through the heroine's eyes.

---

## Chapter 4 -- Udayakumaran Sees Manimekalai

**Literary Function:** Inciting incident.

**Decision:** Expand into a psychologically complex encounter. Udayakumaran should be charismatic, conflicted and human rather than a one-dimensional pursuer.

---

## Chapter 5

Ends Act I.

The appearance of the goddess should feel numinous rather than simply magical.

---

# PART II -- MANIPALLAVAM

Chapters 6--10

**Purpose:** Shift from historical drama into metaphysical discovery.

**Visual language:** Minimal dialogue. Sound becomes sparse. Light replaces spectacle.

---

# PART III -- AMUDHASURABHI

Chapters 11--16

**Purpose:** Transform Manimekalai from seeker into servant of society.

**Central idea:** Compassion is action.

**Decision:** Aputhiran's story will be woven through the narrative rather than isolated.

---

# PART IV -- TRAGEDY

Chapters 17--20

**Purpose:** Every major decision now carries irreversible consequences.

Udayakumaran's death should occur near the midpoint of the film.

---

# PART V -- THE QUEEN

Chapters 21--24

**Purpose:** Political conflict replaces romantic conflict.

**Decision:** Expand Rajamadevi into a tragic antagonist whose grief drives her actions.

---

# PART VI -- PUNNIYARAJAN

Chapters 25--26

**Purpose:** Reveal the larger karmic structure.

**Decision:** Condense while preserving emotional revelations.

---

# PART VII -- DHARMA

Chapters 27--30

**Purpose:** The climax is philosophical rather than military.

**Challenge:** Maintain cinematic tension.

**Decision:** Interweave philosophical debates with the famine in Kanchi so that ideas are tested through human suffering.

---

# Preliminary Three-Act Structure

## Act I

Puhar, festival, love, flight and Manipallavam.

## Act II

Rebirth, Amudhasurabhi, public service, death of Udayakumaran and imprisonment.

## Act III

The Queen, Punniyarajan, Kanchi, philosophical debates and liberation.

---

# Major Adaptation Principles

1. Never modernize the setting.
2. Modernize emotional storytelling.
3. Expand visual storytelling.
4. Reduce exposition.
5. Show philosophy through action whenever possible.
6. Preserve the moral centre of the epic.
